================================================================
KOLKATA METRO — LINE CONNECTIONS & NEAREST BUS STOPS
================================================================
Metro network: Blue (Line 1), Green (Line 2), Orange (Line 6),
               Yellow (Line 4), Purple (Line 3)
Bus stop names sourced from:
  - kolbusopedia.com/bus-routes (private)
  - kolbusopedia.com/bus-routes-government (government)

Note: A metro station and its nearest bus stop usually share a
name or sit at the same crossing. Where the metro station has no
identically named bus stop, the closest stop actually served by
buses is given, with the local landmark in brackets.

================================================================
SECTION 1 — CONNECTIONS BETWEEN METRO LINES (INTERCHANGES)
================================================================

1. NOAPARA
   Blue Line  <->  Yellow Line
   (Yellow Line branches off the Blue Line here toward the Airport.)

2. ESPLANADE
   Blue Line  <->  Green Line
   (Busiest interchange; also the city's central bus hub.)

3. KAVI SUBHASH (New Garia)
   Blue Line  <->  Orange Line
   (Southern terminus shared by both lines.)

Rail interchanges shown on the map (metro <-> Indian Railways):
   - Dum Dum            : Blue Line  <->  Dum Dum Jn (Eastern Rly)
   - Dum Dum Cantonment : Yellow Line <->  Dum Dum Cantonment Rly
   - Noapara            : Blue/Yellow <->  (rail link)
   - Sealdah            : Green Line  <->  Sealdah Station (Eastern Rly)
   - Howrah             : Green Line  <->  Howrah Station (Eastern/SE Rly)
   - Dakshineswar/Baranagar : Blue Line <-> Baranagar-Dakshineswar Rly

The PURPLE LINE (Joka–Majerhat) does not physically meet any
other metro line on this map; it runs as a standalone corridor.
(In the planned full network it will connect at Majerhat.)

================================================================
SECTION 3 — QUICK NOTES
================================================================
- Most metro stations were deliberately named after the
  crossing or locality they sit on, so the metro name and the
  bus stop name are the same word in the large majority of cases.
- The clearest exceptions (renamed metro stations) are on the
  southern Blue Line: "Mahanayak Uttam Kumar" = Tollygunge,
  "Netaji" = Kudghat, "Masterda Surya Sen" = Bansdroni,
  "Gitanjali" = Naktala. Buses still use the old locality names.
- Esplanade, Howrah, Sealdah, Dum Dum and Kavi Subhash double as
  major bus terminals/hubs, so dozens of routes converge there.
- Bus stop spellings vary across operators (e.g. Belgachia /
  Belgacchia, Majherhat / Mahjerhat, Sovabazar / Sova Bazar);
  the spellings above follow the kolbusopedia route listings.
